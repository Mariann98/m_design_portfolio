CrisisSuite

For the course Research & Design, every student had an opportunity to choose between six companies and their design challenges. I chose Merlin Software B.V as my client and their main goal is to help organizations limit the damage in a crisis, by supporting them with practical software tools for crisis management.

Design challenge:

The design challenge was to improve the CrisisSuite software in the warm phase.

Goal:

My main goal with this challenge is to improve the most used and critical tool during a crisis – Logtool. The user can register all the necessary information there which is crucial in a crisis.

Empathize phase

In the define phase, I got to know the client and their product through interviews, desk research, expert review, and competitor analysis. This helped me figure out what feature of the app I want to work on. My first phase of research identified the following pain points related to a feature called Logtool.


  1. In the Logtool there is no way to edit, delete, add attachments, or complete the action in the mobile version, as it is possible in the desktop version.
  2. Everything should be consistent throughout the app, but in Logtool there has been made an exception with adding information. Instead of a “+” button, there is a text box.
  3. In the Logtool the icons and their names (perception, assessment, action, decision) seem unclear. I would like to find out if the icons and descriptions are understandable for the user.

Insights

By the end of this phase I gathered three high-level insights about the Logtool:

  1. It is important to have the same functions as in the desktop version to provide users consistency throughout the software.
  2. The Logtool is missing an easy way for the user to recall actions.
  3. The current user flow is not familiar to the user. Their expectations of certain functions are not applied to the software.
